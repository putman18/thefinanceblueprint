How to Build Credit

By Jordan Hale • Apr 03, 2026 • 8 min read

As a young adult, you're likely no stranger to the concept of credit. You've probably heard that having good credit is important, but maybe you're not entirely sure why or how to get started. The truth is, building credit from scratch can seem daunting, especially if you've never had a credit card or loan before. You might be wondering how to build credit from scratch, and what steps you need to take to establish a strong credit foundation. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of building credit from scratch, and provide you with practical tips and strategies to help you get started.

The problem is, many young adults struggle to get approved for credit cards or loans because they don't have an established credit history. This can make it tough to get started, but there are ways to build credit from scratch. By following the right steps and using the right tools, you can establish a strong credit foundation and start building a positive credit history. In this article, we'll cover the basics of credit, how to get started, and provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to build credit from scratch.

TL;DR / Quick Answer

If you're looking for a quick answer on how to build credit from scratch, here's the basics: apply for a secured credit card or become an authorized user on someone else's account, make regular payments, and monitor your credit report and score. You can also consider using a credit builder loan or a secured loan to help establish credit. Understanding Credit

To build credit from scratch, you need to understand how credit works. Credit refers to the ability to borrow money or access goods and services without paying cash up front. When you apply for credit, the lender will review your credit history to determine whether you're a good candidate for credit. Your credit history is a record of your past borrowing and repayment activities, and it's used to calculate your credit score. A good credit score can help you qualify for better interest rates and terms on credit cards and loans.

Credit Scores

Your credit score is a three-digit number that represents your creditworthiness. It's calculated based on your payment history, credit utilization ratio, length of credit history, and other factors. The most widely used credit score is the FICO score, which ranges from 300 to 850. A good credit score can help you qualify for better interest rates and terms on credit cards and loans, while a poor credit score can make it harder to get approved for credit.

Applying for Credit

To build credit from scratch, you'll need to apply for credit. One of the easiest ways to get started is to apply for a secured credit card. A secured credit card requires a security deposit, which becomes your credit limit. This type of card is designed for people with poor or no credit, and it can help you establish a positive credit history. You can also consider becoming an authorized user on someone else's credit card account. This can help you benefit from their good credit habits and establish a positive credit history.

Choosing the Right Credit Card

When choosing a credit card, consider the following factors: interest rate, fees, credit limit, and rewards. Look for a card with a low interest rate and no annual fee. You should also consider the credit limit and rewards program. Some credit cards offer cash back or travel rewards, which can help you earn money or points for using the card.

Using Credit Responsibly

To build credit from scratch, you need to use credit responsibly. This means making regular payments, keeping your credit utilization ratio low, and avoiding negative marks on your credit report. You should also monitor your credit report and score regularly to ensure that there are no errors or inaccuracies. By using credit responsibly, you can establish a strong credit foundation and improve your credit score over time.

Making Payments

Making regular payments is crucial to building credit from scratch. You should aim to pay your credit card bill in full each month to avoid interest charges. If you can't pay the full amount, make sure to pay at least the minimum payment on time. Late payments can negatively affect your credit score, so it's essential to set up a payment reminder or automate your payments.

Monitoring Your Credit Report and Score

To build credit from scratch, you need to monitor your credit report and score regularly. You can request a free credit report from each of the three major credit bureaus (Experian, TransUnion, and Equifax) once a year. You should also check your credit score regularly to track your progress. You can use a credit monitoring service like Credit Karma or Credit Sesame to get access to your credit score and report.

Disputing Errors

If you find errors or inaccuracies on your credit report, you should dispute them immediately. You can contact the credit bureau directly to dispute the error, and they will investigate and correct it if necessary. Disputing errors can help improve your credit score and ensure that your credit report is accurate.
